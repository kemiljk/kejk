DUCHENNE AND ME

WHO WAS IT FOR?

Duchenne is an extremely rare condition affecting around 1 in 3,500 children. It is due to a defective chromosome that stops proteins from being created correctly. As a result, muscle tissue turns to fat and often the child only lives until their mid-20’s. These children, due to their restrictions, are often very computer savvy and tend to love playing video games and using computers.

The other side of the coin is the parents and carers of these children. Although not directly affected by the condition, they are tasked with managing the day to day condition for the child and tend to struggle to do so.

WHAT CHALLENGES DID THEY HAVE?

From research we conducted with parents and carers, we found overwhelmingly that 100% of them struggled to remember to track vital statistics about their child (growth, weight gain, activity levels) and remember to provide them with the correct doses of their medications at the right times (often the mid-day dose was most challenging to remember). Secondly, they struggle in emergency situations such as visiting A&E. The condition is so rare that most emergency staff never see a case and as such aren’t aware of specific difficulties such as easy fracturing of bones or limited lung capacity when put on ventilators.

WHAT MIGHT THEY NEED?

All parents in our research group agreed that some kind of application was ideal for keeping on top of things both in and out of the home. As they, and their children, used smart devices regularly it made sense to go to where they already are. We needed to ensure that whatever we built was cross-platform and worked across a wide-range of devices due to the need to deliver across many countries worldwide.

WHAT DID WE DO?

Following research and sharing of paper prototypes, we found the sweet spot for the child and parent/carer’s needs was to have sections for the following:

  • Dashboard (for showing key child statistics)
  • Schedule (with pre- and post-appointment reminders/notes)
  • Emergency care considerations (for A&E situations)
  • Medication (for reminders and accurate dosing)
  • Help (for frequently asked questions, delivered as a sql-based chat bot)

Platform

In order to deliver something scalable and cross-platform we opted for an app that we could deploy through Appcelerator to iOS and Android. This meant we needed to focus on JavaScript as our main language of choice and use JSON and XML to handle language translations.

Rollout

Duchenne and Me is currently live in 3 countries; the UK, Germany and Turkey, and is due to go live in several other countries in the coming months. Reception so far has been excellent and app retention remains at around 83%.

DUCHENNE AND YOU / TAKE ON DUCHENNE

WHO WAS IT FOR?

Duchenne is an extremely rare condition affecting around 1 in 3,500 children. It is due to a defective chromosome that stops proteins from being created correctly.

Resources for parents and healthcare professionals are hard to come by when it comes to rare disease and our client saw a need for providing a wealth of information to both audiences.

WHAT CHALLENGES DID THEY HAVE?

One of the biggest factors we were made aware of through parent interviews was the rarity of the disease and the likelihood that a child didn’t have Duchenne. The worry, is that it’s easy as a frightened parent of a young child displaying normal symptoms of slow development to see Duchenne as a possible answer and be terrified at seeing their child will die young. This potential scaremongering is something our client wished to avoid at all costs.

Healthcare professionals on the other hand struggled with a lack of high-science information for them to read and understand. A GP will likely never see a case in their lifetime but if they did, they’d need ample resource to help them.

WHAT MIGHT THEY NEED?

The conversations led us to believe that a platform needed to exist online with parent and HCP-friendly information on it. It needed to focus heavily on showing only the most important and relevant information to someone when they needed it.

WHAT DID WE DO?

The outcome was a pair of websites that had a share information architecture and interface paradigm. They leveraged a collapsed nav by default at all screen sizes (to reduce the urge to skip to the “worst case scenario”), deeper content hidden behind pop ups that are also bullet points and section footers that encouraged moving to the next section when they’d read the whole site (thus confirming the content was still relevant).

Platform

HTML, CSS, JavaScript and PHP hosted through Wordpress.

Rollout

Duchenne and You is currently live in Europe, the US, Spain, Germany and Portugal. Take on Duchenne is currently live in Europe.

GENETIC DISEASES PLAYBOOK

WHO WAS IT FOR?

Internal stakeholders at a pharmaceutical company across a variety of experience levels

WHAT CHALLENGES DID THEY HAVE?

The disease can be spotted by any of 6 different specialist doctors depending on what symptoms a patient is presenting with. Because of this, different markets have been targeting different specialists with their branded and unbranded materials to raise awareness of the disease. The challenge was that each market was focussed in different areas and therefore it was hard to measure global progress. Some of these specialities were easier to target than others, so there were quick wins being missed by several markets. Our client needed a tool to help solve this.

The other major challenge was that the assessments used to measure success were being kept in multiple Excel spreadsheets that weren’t centralised or easily locatable.

WHAT MIGHT THEY NEED?

Our core stakeholders all had laptops and smartphones provided by the company, some also had iPads if their role was in sales. The challenge was that this tool would require updating in many scenarios, so whatever the tool was, it needed to be multi-device and fully scalable.

WHAT DID WE DO?

We built a progressive web app that was fully scalable. It allowed the client to access the content at any location, on any of the three devices they’d want to. It was also vitally important that they could fill out the assessments without resorting to using another platform or software, so we built the tool to use a sql database to capture the answers and this allowed us to build algorithms to measure success based on baseline metrics and provide insights to the users on possible next steps.

Platform

HTML, CSS, JavaScript.

Rollout

This tool is global and so is held centrally but in use on secure servers across the entire world.

DRY EYE DISEASE APP

WHO WAS IT FOR?

Key opinion leaders who are experts in diagnosing dry eye disease.

WHAT CHALLENGES DID THEY HAVE?

Keratitis (dry eye disease) is very rare but extremely progressive. Currently, general practitioners struggle to diagnose the severity early enough and monitor that progression over time. As a result, patients are diagnosed too late or rarely improve.

Current methods involve reviewing physical symptoms based on both experience and a paper chart called the Oxford Rating Scale. Both of these methods are only as effective as the doctor's experience and as such often lead to misdiagnoses and lack of accurate progression tracking.

THE OXFORD RATING SCALE

WHAT MIGHT THEY NEED?

A tool to help support correct diagnoses for these non-expert audiences. The experts are comfortable in diagnoses but they aren't always consulted by GPs when diagnoses are carried out. There have been several papers testing out the idea of a machine learning algorithm that can detect the condition by counting the visual coverage of the abrasions on the eye. This validated the approach of building a standalone application for dry eye disease as a first-to-market product.

A fluorescent dye is placed in the patient's eye and these are then photographed under UV light to see the extent of the abrasions. The end result is an image that can be compared against pre-scored expert examples in a database of images.

WHAT DID WE DO?

We built a prototype for an application that can take images of an eye using a smartphone camera and compare the severity of the condition against a database of previously scored images. The expert can then either approve the machine score or assign a new score. The tool was decided to be an application for smartphones and tablets (some level of scalability) and is planned to evolve to eventually have Class I medical device status so it’s able to preemptively diagnose based on imaging.

Platform

React or Vue.js for iOS and Android.

Rollout

This tool would be globally released for all medical professionals following a beta test period with experts.
