DUCHENNE AND ME

WHO WAS IT FOR?

Duchenne is an extremely rare condition affecting around 1 in 3,500 children. It is due to a defective chromosome that stops proteins from being created correctly. As a result, muscle tissue turns to fat and often the child only lives until their mid-20’s. These children, due to their restrictions, are often very computer savvy and tend to love playing video games and using computers.

The other side of the coin is the parents and carers of these children. Although not directly affected by the condition, they are tasked with managing the day to day condition for the child and tend to struggle to do so.

WHAT CHALLENGES DID THEY HAVE?

From research we conducted with parents and carers, we found overwhelmingly that 100% of them struggled to remember to track vital statistics about their child (growth, weight gain, activity levels) and remember to provide them with the correct doses of their medications at the right times (often the mid-day dose was most challenging to remember). Secondly, they struggle in emergency situations such as visiting A&E. The condition is so rare that most emergency staff never see a case and as such aren’t aware of specific difficulties such as easy fracturing of bones or limited lung capacity when put on ventilators.

WHAT MIGHT THEY NEED?

All parents in our research group agreed that some kind of application was ideal for keeping on top of things both in and out of the home. As they, and their children, used smart devices regularly it made sense to go to where they already are. We needed to ensure that whatever we built was cross-platform and worked across a wide-range of devices due to the need to deliver across many countries worldwide.

WHAT DID WE DO?

Following research and sharing of paper prototypes, we found the sweet spot for the child and parent/carer’s needs was to have sections for the following:

  • Dashboard (for showing key child statistics)
  • Schedule (with pre- and post-appointment reminders/notes)
  • Emergency care considerations (for A&E situations)
  • Medication (for reminders and accurate dosing)
  • Help (for frequently asked questions, delivered as a sql-based chat bot)

Platform

In order to deliver something scalable and cross-platform we opted for an app that we could deploy through Appcelerator. This meant we needed to focus on JavaScript as our main language of choice and use JSON and XML to handle language translations.

Rollout

Duchenne and Me is currently live in 3 countries; the UK, Germany and Turkey, and is due to go live in several other countries in the coming months. Reception so far has been excellent and app retention remains at around 83%.
