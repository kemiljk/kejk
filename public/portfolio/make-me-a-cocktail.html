Make Me a Cocktail

What is it?

Make me a cocktail (MMAC) is an online resource where at it's core users can enter what ingredients they have and get shown what they can make with the said ingredients. It utilises a clever substitution algorithm to broaden users results while still allowing cocktails to be exact and accurate.

The website contains over 3000 cocktails to be searched over, with clear instructions and pictures to help users make the best cocktails they can.

My role

I was hired by Nick Wilkins, CEO of Make Me a Cocktail directly to work on refining the design of the new version 5 build of the website. Nick and his team had spent a lot of time developing a new core user journey along with a substantial update in technology and architecture. The team wanted my focus to be on a few core pages and ensure they retained the existing look and feel but with the opportunity to add some new elements of design.

During my website assessment, I noticed a glaring need for refining and updating the website design system to create a more unified and modern look and feel without losing the existing design language.

My role consisted of the following:

  • Working with the site owner to identify current pain points and opportunities for improvement
  • Identification and consolidation of core requirements
  • Analysis of current website data and new user journey
  • Creation of low-fidelity wireframes
  • Creation of high-fidelity wireframes and prototype
  • Creation of full designs, including a new design system and component set
  • Provision of files to development

Summary (the TL;DR version)

Make Me a Cocktail is a very successful website that caters to a wide audience of cocktail lovers, providing a much needed single source of truth when looking for cocktail recipes.

I was approached to provide guidance on the user experience and final design of several new pages following a major version 5 update. It was important for me to maintain the existing design language but I was given creative license to adapt and advance the existing platform. During the design stage I felt it necessary to start to think about developing a new design system, one that create greater harmony to the elements on a page, one that unified the interface language through consistent use of a bespoke icon set, one that ensured the most optimal experience across desktop and mobile.

The success of these three page updates has led to me securing an ongoing agreement to support further development of the site from both an advisory and designer's perspective.

Version 5 is set to go live soon.

Who was it for?

Cocktail lovers who are new to using Make Me a Cocktail. Existing Make Me a Cocktail users who deserve a consistent and delightful experience.

What challenges did they have?

The current website was undergoing maintenance so it didn't feature the core pages I was asked to produce. It did, in some ways, have pages that were similar to existing pages where users could form lists of favourite cocktails, specific lists for events/cocktails/menus. These pages needed to be rethought to make a better user experience.

The Getting Started experience required a completely new thought process. This page's focus was on trying to make the onboarding experience for a new user better as currently it can be quite overwhelming.

What might they need?

Both existing and new users need similar things in this instance, the focus was on making the Getting Started experience simple and easy. The brief I was given included a list of basic requirements for the page and nothing more. The MMAC team gave me a strong brief, including all of the relevant information I needed to get started but the actual direction was left up to me. As stated earlier, it was very important that I maintained the current design language but made some minor evolutions, so I chose to focus on adding some new design elements such as; the title banners for the four quick jump sections, the new button styles for section links and the new icon-set that I developed.

I discovered the following in my assessment:

  • A lack of consistency in the icon-set
  • A sharpness to the interface elements
  • Opportunities for new little-big details (moments of delight)

What did I do?

I developed a series of wireframes and interface designs to provide the MMAC team with the elements they need to be able to produce the updated interface pages. This included a full set of wireframes, designs and components for building the new interface. The intention is that this will form the next set of interface updates throughout the rest of the website before the next full version 6 update.
